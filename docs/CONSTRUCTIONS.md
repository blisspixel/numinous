# Constructions: the puzzle layer with a par, a ghost, and elegance

The game spine. This doc turns each room's optional Puzzle from a graded probe
into a real, replayable construction with a par and an elegance score, the way
Euclidea and the Zachtronics games make a puzzle bottomless. It is Phase C of
`NORTH_STAR.md`. `PROGRESSION.md` stays the philosophy (knowledge is the only
reward); this is the spec that philosophy needs. The daily route that strings
constructions together lives in `CONSTELLATION.md`.

## The diagnosis

The three-layer model (Toy, Puzzle, Revelation) is designed, but in the build the
Puzzle layer is mostly the `challenge` tool: a graded 0-100 touch or parameter
goal. That is a scoring probe, not a puzzle with a par. A room whose "whoa" you
have seen has nothing pulling you back tomorrow. Thirty rooms of look-and-leave
are thirty shallow wells, not one deep one. The fix is not more rooms; it is
giving the rooms we have a floor you can descend.

## The mechanic: three stars, a histogram, a ghost

Promote each room's Puzzle to a named **construction** with tiered goals, in the
Euclidea mold (which scores every level three ways: a completion star, a fewest-
moves star, and a fewest-elementary-moves star, plus a variant star for finding
every solution):

- **Solve it** (the kid tier): make the math answer the goal at all. This is the
  existing 0-100 touch grade, surfaced as a gentle climbing number, not a fail.
- **Solve it in par** (the L-star): hit the target in the fewest moves, the
  closest tune, or within tolerance. This is where an adult finds flow.
- **Solve it minimally, the masterpiece** (the E-star): the cleanest solution.
  The histogram never bottoms out, because someone always has a tighter one.

Results show as a **local histogram**, never a global leaderboard. The
Zachtronics reason is decisive: a global leaderboard "is a fantastic incentive
for cheating, and for most players the only thing it tells you is that you suck,"
while a histogram lets you "quickly and impersonally see how your solution stacks
up" (SpaceChem postmortem). The histogram is the "one more" engine: players
re-solve the same puzzle three different ways because the cheapest, fastest, and
smallest solutions are usually different designs. That is difficulty on demand,
generated by the player, for free, forever.

**The ghost of your past self.** Your best attempt replays as a translucent
overlay while you try again. Self-competition is the purest "one more" with zero
social downside, and it solves the histogram's cold-start problem completely: on
a fresh offline install the histogram is empty, but you always have yourself.
Watching your understanding visibly sharpen (a jerkier ghost becoming your smooth
present hand) is felt progress that is real, not cosmetic. The gesture trail
(`RoomInput`, phase-stamped) already records everything needed to replay a hand,
so this is among the cheapest big wins in the plan.

## The cost metric is per room, the presentation is shared

"Elegance" means something different in each Wing, and forcing a universal score
would be a lie. So the **cost metric is bespoke** and the **presentation is
unified**:

| Room family | The cost metric (what "elegant" minimizes) |
|---|---|
| Construction geometry | tool moves, then elementary moves |
| Studio and function rooms | expression length or term count |
| Fraction and number rooms | denominator size, digit count |
| Cellular and agent rooms | cells touched, steps used |
| Tuning rooms (Lissajous, Harmonograph) | closeness to the exact ratio in fewest adjustments |

All of them read through the same widget: the tiered goals, the par, the local
histogram, the ghost. The currency reads consistent without a fake universal
number. This resolves the "shared vs bespoke elegance metric" open question in
`PROGRESSION.md`: bespoke metric, shared presentation.

## Cold-start honesty

A local histogram is empty on a fresh, offline, single-player install. Two
defenses, both required:

1. **A seeded par curve per construction**, authored by the designer with AI
   assistance: a distribution a solo player climbs from move one, so there is
   always a curve even with no community corpus.
2. **The ghost**, which needs no corpus at all: you are always your own baseline.

Neither depends on a server or other players, so the game layer ships fully
in single-player before any community exists.

## The no-fail invariant, extended

The existing rule (a wrong answer is never punished, only taught from) extends to
constructions: **a par you miss is never a loss.** You solved it; you simply have
not yet solved it beautifully. The histogram shows where you stand impersonally,
and the only opponent that can "beat" you is who you were. No streak debt, no
name-and-shame, no cheating incentive. This is the anti-casino soul of
`AGENT_PLAY.md` preserved inside a real difficulty system.

## Difficulty on demand: one room, curious kid to expert

The design principle: the Toy is the floor and never moves; difficulty is a
ladder the player climbs voluntarily, and the same interaction is legible at
every rung. Four rungs on every room, all reading the same poke:

- **Rung 0, Toy.** Poke it, it answers, sound plays. No goal, no score. Untouched.
- **Rung 1, Make-it-move.** The 0-100 touch grade surfaced as a bar a kid wants
  to push up ("you moved the math, 62, can you hit 80?"). Difficulty the kid sets
  by trying harder, not by reading.
- **Rung 2, Par.** The L-star, where an adult finds flow. Because the challenge
  target boxes are placed on the room's measured densest response, every par is
  winnable by construction (already proven with a registry witness test), so
  nobody hits an impossible wall.
- **Rung 3, Masterpiece and insight.** The minimal solution, the actively-
  confirmed reveal edge (see `CONSTELLATION.md`), and authoring your own dare
  (below). The expert lives here; the histogram's left tail never empties.

The histogram and the ghost generate the difficulty for free: you do not
hand-author 30 difficulty curves, you hand players a distribution and let them
choose their target. That is the only way difficulty on demand scales to 30-plus
rooms without a per-room content explosion.

## Beyond the puzzle: performance mastery

Elegance-scored constructions give a room a skill floor, but the deepest rooms
should also have a **performance** ceiling, in the instrument sense of
`VISION.md`. The question is not only "can the player solve this in par" but
"can the player get good at *playing* this room": improvise a trajectory,
develop timing and rhythm, build a recognizable style another player could
identify. Times Tables today is "drag the multiplier"; its performance form is
an expert executing an evolving multiplier trajectory where speed, rhythm, and
the resulting sound all matter, and "my best Lorenz solo" is a coherent,
shareable artifact. This is the honest, uncapped alternative to an XP treadmill
(see `SCOPE.md`, "Mastery is math's, not XP's"), and it is where a room stops
being a puzzle you finish and becomes an instrument you never do. Not every
room needs a performance ceiling, but the flagship and the continuous ones
(Times Tables, Lorenz, Harmonograph, the Studio) should aim for one.

## Self-set challenge, the top rung

Let a player or agent author a seeded challenge (the `challenge` tool already
poses both touch and parameter goals) and export it as a **dare card**: a `.num`
or `numinous://` payload that opens the exact board and goal, carrying the
sharer's result to beat. This is serverless asynchronous multiplayer, because the
seed is the network; it closes the "social mutuality" gap without the 2.0 network
service; and it makes authoring, the deepest form of engagement (Papert's
constructionism), a first-class act. The difficulty ceiling becomes the
community's imagination, not our content budget.

## Test hooks

- Every construction is winnable by construction (extend the existing
  registry-wide challenge witness test to the par tier).
- Ghost replay is deterministic: the same recorded trail renders identically.
- No collectible without a lesson: a construction never grants a
  Constellation node without a real solve (the generation-act rule from
  `PEDAGOGY.md`).

## Honest tradeoffs

- Histograms need a corpus; solved by the seeded par curve and the ghost, at a
  real per-room authoring cost.
- Roguelike runs (the daily route in `CONSTELLATION.md`) risk becoming the XP
  treadmill `PROGRESSION.md` bans; defused only if the reward stays knowledge and
  the run stays bounded. This is a written invariant, not a hope.
