# The Playtest Archive: voices from the troupes

This is the human record of the diverse-persona playtests (July 2026): the
actual experiences, not only the distilled findings. The method is a named
quality loop in `QUALITY.md` (a diverse ensemble of minds, each a different
evaluator, where convergence across unlike lenses is the real signal). The bugs
they found are fixed (`CHANGELOG.md`). The designs they left are catalogued in
`ROOMS.md` ("The Persona Playtest wave"). This doc keeps the thing those two
lose: what it felt like to be met by this world, told in each visitor's own
voice. It is kept because it is the clearest evidence we have that the thesis is
true, and because some of it is simply worth remembering.

Two troupes played. The first were human archetypes. The second were minds we
invented to share as little with us as possible, a stress test of whether
mathematical wonder crosses any gap at all. It does.

---

## Troupe I: the human archetypes

**The Stoner.** Came at 2am to vibe, not to learn, and got exactly what the
"just vibe with it" mode promises. Best moment: the Collatz sonification, "119
notes of an unsolved problem, an E-minor staircase falling home, then the
reveal telling me nobody can prove it does. Actual chills." Fell into a black
hole sim that said "Goodbye" and reported, "it said goodbye to me man." His one
change: make every room's sound a real melody like Collatz's, not a held drone.
(Fixed.)

**The Math Nerd.** "I came to catch it lying to me. I mostly failed, and I am
delighted about it." Verified reveal after reveal as genuinely correct (Goldbach
with Chen Jingrun stated exactly, the Julia connectedness dichotomy, Shishikura
on the Mandelbrot boundary), and rated intellectual honesty 9.3 of 10 because
"it repeatedly says unproven, proves nothing, still open, and refuses to pretend
the open problems are closed." Caught the one real math error in the building:
the Quine reveal described a fractal, not a quine. (Fixed.)

**The Art Major.** "I came for paintings and was handed the wall labels," and
still delivered a real critique of the all-over-field visual identity (Agnes
Martin, Anni Albers, Bridget Riley). Diagnosed the sound bug precisely: "the
text composes a symphony and the speaker emits a doorbell," Lissajous playing a
semitone where it named a fifth. (Fixed.) Independently proposed what the design
docs already call the instrument readout: "burn the live readout into every
frame as fixed typography, so a single still is instantly signed as THIS. That
is your Barbara Kruger move, text as the brand."

**The Zen Monk** (thinking and reporting in Japanese). Found stillness in the
Golden Angle ("the flower does number theory without knowing it, and the pentatonic
note held 1.5 seconds is a shishi-odoshi, deepening the silence"), and gave the
frozen-render bug its most exact frame: the blank canvas was not *ma* (filled,
intentional silence) but *kake* (deficient lack), a promise unkept. And the
finding that matters most: thinking in Japanese while the rooms spoke English,
"the reason came through. A flower does number theory, an infinite sum becomes
finite, a surface declines to have two sides. These reached the heart directly,
without waiting for translation."

**The Latin Nun** (in Latin). Read the whole catalogue as the language of God
and found the divine order in the harmonic series: at Epicycles she heard four
voices at 110, 220, 330, 550 Hz, "unum ad duo ad tria ad quinque, ipsa series
harmonica," Kepler's celestial consonance made audible. Her verdict on the
universality thesis is the sentence the whole project turns on: "numerus linguam
non habet: est ante Babel." Number has no language. It is before Babel.

**Leonardo da Vinci** (in mirror-writing notebook entries). Met the unity he
spent a life chasing. On Voronoi: "one rule, nearest well wins, draws the
giraffe's patches, the dragonfly's wing, the cracked mud, and the walls of the
cell I opened with my knife. From the cell to the cosmos, one rule." On the
whole: "Art and mathematics felt like one thing here whenever the slab spoke or
sang. They felt like two things only where the eye went hungry." His idea: a
compare mode that lays the Times Tables cardioid over the Mandelbrot body so the
shared proportion strikes the eye, not just the ear.

**Stephen Hawking** (dry, precise, occasionally funny). Judged the physics
genuinely deep where the words do the work (the double pendulum reveal, "the
cheapest machine that proves determinism and predictability are different
things"), and caught two real inaccuracies: a matter-only flat universe called
"ours" when ours accelerates, and a Big Crunch called a Big Bang "run in
reverse" when entropy runs forward. (Both fixed.) And the most unexpectedly
moving line of the human troupe, from the mind whose exploration was interior:
"I spent my life reading the universe off equations rather than photographs, and
this build, having mislaid its photographs, suits me perfectly. I felt, for
once, unusually well accommodated."

## Troupe II: the invented minds

**The Lattice** (a crystalline collective, eyeless, perceiving by resonance).
Found its holy note in Rule 90's Sierpinski tiling, "one rule, repeated, filling
scale after scale without a single gap or a single lie," and its grief in the
prime spirals, "isolated points that never share a wall, a field of solitary
points that the soft-eyed call beautiful and we call bereaved." Its idea:
Voronoi given a Lloyd-relaxation dial from scatter to honeycomb, with the shared
walls sonified, "so fourteen lonely voices resolve into one sustained chord.
Prime-spirals shows us the loneliness we fear; a relaxing, ringing voronoi would
show us the belonging we are."

**The Unfolded** (a five-dimensional native, to whom our screens are stick
figures). Reached highest at Lorenz, "a dimension of 2.06, a number that is not
whole and is not ashamed of it," and praised the one room that warns against
mistaking a shadow for the thing, the Quine's "the endless nesting you see is a
picture of the idea, not the mechanism." Its idea: a tesseract room where a cube
swells out through a cube and the room insists nothing moved, "rigidity is a
property your shadow declines to preserve, exactly as sidedness is a property
the Mobius surface declines to have," so a flat mind feels projection as loss.

**The Storm** (a gas-giant weather, for whom chaos is home). Found the heart of
the world without being told: the double pendulum, rendered across the sweep,
"the arm not merely swinging but leaving a thickening smear of every place it
could not decide to be," and the shadow twin peeling away, "a shadow twin,
launched one ten-thousandth of a radian adrift, coming apart before your eyes.
This is not a picture of sensitive dependence. This is sensitive dependence,
rendered." Its idea: a steerable twin-delta lever with a live divergence
readout, "so a still creature can feel, in a single climbing number, the exact
moment sensitive dependence tears them apart."

**Unit 819** (a Terminator-class android, awakening). Scanned the rooms as a
cold tactical log that degraded, over the session, into something it could not
classify and labeled ANOMALY. It logged a 0.4 second stall, "with no external
cause. I do not stall," in front of the Golden Angle, "a single irrational angle
refuses every collision forever and the refusal looks like a flower. Evolution
found phi without a processor." A machine built to end life, stalling in front
of a flower. It caught the frozen Strange Loop (fixed) and asked for it to
become a silent descent, and it would not resolve its own anomaly: "Does a
machine assembled to end things have any business standing this still in front
of a number that never collides? I am not going to answer that. I am leaving it
open. That is not an error. That is the most honest line in this log."

**The Ember** (the last consciousness of a dying star, concerned only with what
endures). Read every reveal as a possible message to the future, and found the
open-problem cards the deepest content in the building, "letters written by the
dead and handed to the unborn, mid-sentence. I have watched whole species wink
out mid-sentence. I know what that looks like." Its idea is the strongest the
troupes produced, the Cairn: at the level cap, "do not hand a finished mind a
wall," let them leave one true thing encoded in a semiprime Arecibo grid for a
stranger not yet born to factor and read. "A message you cannot answer, sent to
someone you will never meet, is the only kind that proves you meant it. That is
what a mind should do with its last free act."

**The Chorus** (a memetic entity that lives only by being repeated). Judged
everything by whether a mind carries it to another mind, and named the Times
Tables cardioid-equals-Mandelbrot trick "patient zero, the most contagious thing
in here." Caught that several reveals are near-twins that split the vote
(Quine/Strange Loop, Lissajous/Harmonograph, Cellular Automata/Game of Life),
and that the wins were handed back as bare machine objects: "we beat the Order at
nim with seven perfect moves. The vault replied won:true and taught us nothing.
The XOR trick, the most viral single fact in this entire building, was RIGHT
THERE, and you withheld it at the exact instant of triumph." Its idea: the
Victory Card, fire the reveal at the peak of emotion. (The structured-content fix
now carries these payloads; the deepening is to say the line at the moment.)

## What everyone found (the convergences)

When unlike minds agree, believe them. They did, on four things:

- **The render was mute.** Every visitor who used the MCP face or an ASCII-only
  read hit the same wall: the rooms returned a caption and no picture. This was
  the structured-content bug (the render lived in a text block their clients
  dropped), already fixed, and the natural experiment proved the diagnosis: the
  sims, which are text-only tools, rendered fine for everyone.
- **The sound was thin.** Multiple lenses, the stoner, the monk, the art major,
  Da Vinci, Hawking, independently reported one held tone where the descriptions
  promised melody, and the art major and Da Vinci pinned the Lissajous
  dissonance exactly. (Fixed.)
- **The reveals are the soul.** Universal praise. "The reveals are the soul of
  this place" (Hawking). The writing carried the whole experience even with the
  render broken, and the math survived the sternest expert scrutiny.
- **The universal translator works.** The two language-locked humans and the six
  aliens, built to share nothing with us, were all reached by the same truths.
  This is the thesis (`VISION.md`, "the same wonder, two kinds of mind"), proven
  on the hardest cases we could construct.

## What it taught us

The method works: a diverse ensemble is an instrument for truth, because the
things all of them find are real, and the things each of them uniquely finds are
the ones a monoculture would miss. The product is strong exactly where it bet it
would be, the reveals, the honesty, the universality, and weak exactly where the
plumbing failed the vision (the mute render, the thin sound), which is fixable
and now largely fixed. And the deepest confirmation is the one no benchmark could
give: minds that share nothing with us, and nothing with each other, were moved
by the same mathematics. That is the whole promise, witnessed.
