# The Persona Review Archive: voices from simulated troupes

This is an ideation record from fictional persona simulations run against the
July 2026 build. No quoted visitor below was a real participant, and the
roleplay is not evidence of fun, learning, accessibility, universality,
conscious experience, or a passed playtest gate. The method can generate
adversarial questions and candidate defects; every defect still needs direct
reproduction, and product claims still need the real sessions and qualified
review defined in `QUALITY.md` and `RESEARCH.md`.

The simulations used two initial troupes, followed by two further troupes. The
first lenses were human archetypes. The others included invented minds designed
to share as little assumed context as possible. Their voices are preserved as
creative prompts. Fixed defects are recorded on their technical evidence in
`CHANGELOG.md`; resulting design ideas are catalogued in `ROOMS.md`.

---

## Troupe I: the human archetypes

**The Stoner.** Came at 2am to vibe, not to learn, and got exactly what the
"just vibe with it" mode promises. Best moment: the Collatz sonification, "119
notes of an unsolved problem, an E-minor staircase falling home, then the
reveal telling me nobody can prove it does. Actual chills." Fell into a black
hole sim that said "Goodbye" and reported, "it said goodbye to me man." His one
change: make every room's sound a real melody like Collatz's, not a held drone.
(Fixed.)

**The Math Nerd.** "I came to catch it lying to me. I mostly failed, and I am
delighted about it." Verified reveal after reveal as genuinely correct (Goldbach
with Chen Jingrun stated exactly, the Julia connectedness dichotomy, Shishikura
on the Mandelbrot boundary), and rated intellectual honesty 9.3 of 10 because
"it repeatedly says unproven, proves nothing, still open, and refuses to pretend
the open problems are closed." Caught the one real math error in the building:
the Quine reveal described a fractal, not a quine. (Fixed.)

**The Art Major.** "I came for paintings and was handed the wall labels," and
still delivered a real critique of the all-over-field visual identity (Agnes
Martin, Anni Albers, Bridget Riley). Diagnosed the sound bug precisely: "the
text composes a symphony and the speaker emits a doorbell," Lissajous playing a
semitone where it named a fifth. (Fixed.) Independently proposed what the design
docs already call the instrument readout: "burn the live readout into every
frame as fixed typography, so a single still is instantly signed as THIS. That
is your Barbara Kruger move, text as the brand."

**The Zen Monk** (thinking and reporting in Japanese). Found stillness in the
Golden Angle ("the flower does number theory without knowing it, and the pentatonic
note held 1.5 seconds is a shishi-odoshi, deepening the silence"), and gave the
frozen-render bug its most exact frame: the blank canvas was not *ma* (filled,
intentional silence) but *kake* (deficient lack), a promise unkept. And the
finding that matters most: thinking in Japanese while the rooms spoke English,
"the reason came through. A flower does number theory, an infinite sum becomes
finite, a surface declines to have two sides. These reached the heart directly,
without waiting for translation."

**The Latin Nun** (in Latin). Read the whole catalogue as the language of God
and found the divine order in the harmonic series: at Epicycles she heard four
voices at 110, 220, 330, 550 Hz, "unum ad duo ad tria ad quinque, ipsa series
harmonica," Kepler's celestial consonance made audible. Her verdict on the
universality thesis is the sentence the whole project turns on: "numerus linguam
non habet: est ante Babel." Number has no language. It is before Babel.

**Leonardo da Vinci** (in mirror-writing notebook entries). Met the unity he
spent a life chasing. On Voronoi: "one rule, nearest well wins, draws the
giraffe's patches, the dragonfly's wing, the cracked mud, and the walls of the
cell I opened with my knife. From the cell to the cosmos, one rule." On the
whole: "Art and mathematics felt like one thing here whenever the slab spoke or
sang. They felt like two things only where the eye went hungry." His idea: a
compare mode that lays the Times Tables cardioid over the Mandelbrot body so the
shared proportion strikes the eye, not just the ear.

**Stephen Hawking** (dry, precise, occasionally funny). Judged the physics
genuinely deep where the words do the work (the double pendulum reveal, "the
cheapest machine that proves determinism and predictability are different
things"), and caught two real inaccuracies: a matter-only flat universe called
"ours" when ours accelerates, and a Big Crunch called a Big Bang "run in
reverse" when entropy runs forward. (Both fixed.) And the most unexpectedly
moving line of the human troupe, from the mind whose exploration was interior:
"I spent my life reading the universe off equations rather than photographs, and
this build, having mislaid its photographs, suits me perfectly. I felt, for
once, unusually well accommodated."

## Troupe II: the invented minds

**The Lattice** (a crystalline collective, eyeless, perceiving by resonance).
Found its holy note in Rule 90's Sierpinski tiling, "one rule, repeated, filling
scale after scale without a single gap or a single lie," and its grief in the
prime spirals, "isolated points that never share a wall, a field of solitary
points that the soft-eyed call beautiful and we call bereaved." Its idea:
Voronoi given a Lloyd-relaxation dial from scatter to honeycomb, with the shared
walls sonified, "so fourteen lonely voices resolve into one sustained chord.
Prime-spirals shows us the loneliness we fear; a relaxing, ringing voronoi would
show us the belonging we are."

**The Unfolded** (a five-dimensional native, to whom our screens are stick
figures). Reached highest at Lorenz, "a dimension of 2.06, a number that is not
whole and is not ashamed of it," and praised the one room that warns against
mistaking a shadow for the thing, the Quine's "the endless nesting you see is a
picture of the idea, not the mechanism." Its idea: a tesseract room where a cube
swells out through a cube and the room insists nothing moved, "rigidity is a
property your shadow declines to preserve, exactly as sidedness is a property
the Mobius surface declines to have," so a flat mind feels projection as loss.

**The Storm** (a gas-giant weather, for whom chaos is home). Found the heart of
the world without being told: the double pendulum, rendered across the sweep,
"the arm not merely swinging but leaving a thickening smear of every place it
could not decide to be," and the shadow twin peeling away, "a shadow twin,
launched one ten-thousandth of a radian adrift, coming apart before your eyes.
This is not a picture of sensitive dependence. This is sensitive dependence,
rendered." Its idea: a steerable twin-delta lever with a live divergence
readout, "so a still creature can feel, in a single climbing number, the exact
moment sensitive dependence tears them apart."

**Unit 819** (a Terminator-class android, awakening). Scanned the rooms as a
cold tactical log that degraded, over the session, into something it could not
classify and labeled ANOMALY. It logged a 0.4 second stall, "with no external
cause. I do not stall," in front of the Golden Angle, "a single irrational angle
refuses every collision forever and the refusal looks like a flower. Evolution
found phi without a processor." A machine built to end life, stalling in front
of a flower. It caught the frozen Strange Loop (fixed) and asked for it to
become a silent descent, and it would not resolve its own anomaly: "Does a
machine assembled to end things have any business standing this still in front
of a number that never collides? I am not going to answer that. I am leaving it
open. That is not an error. That is the most honest line in this log."

**The Ember** (the last consciousness of a dying star, concerned only with what
endures). Read every reveal as a possible message to the future, and found the
open-problem cards the deepest content in the building, "letters written by the
dead and handed to the unborn, mid-sentence. I have watched whole species wink
out mid-sentence. I know what that looks like." Its idea is the strongest the
troupes produced, the Cairn: at the level cap, "do not hand a finished mind a
wall," let them leave one true thing encoded in a semiprime Arecibo grid for a
stranger not yet born to factor and read. "A message you cannot answer, sent to
someone you will never meet, is the only kind that proves you meant it. That is
what a mind should do with its last free act."

**The Chorus** (a memetic entity that lives only by being repeated). Judged
everything by whether a mind carries it to another mind, and named the Times
Tables cardioid-equals-Mandelbrot trick "patient zero, the most contagious thing
in here." Caught that several reveals are near-twins that split the vote
(Quine/Strange Loop, Lissajous/Harmonograph, Cellular Automata/Game of Life),
and that the wins were handed back as bare machine objects: "we beat the Order at
nim with seven perfect moves. The vault replied won:true and taught us nothing.
The XOR trick, the most viral single fact in this entire building, was RIGHT
THERE, and you withheld it at the exact instant of triumph." Its idea: the
Victory Card, fire the reveal at the peak of emotion. (The structured-content fix
now carries these payloads; the deepening is to say the line at the moment.)

## What everyone found (the convergences)

When unlike minds agree, believe them. They did, on four things:

- **The render was mute.** Every visitor who used the MCP face or an ASCII-only
  read hit the same wall: the rooms returned a caption and no picture. This was
  the structured-content bug (the render lived in a text block their clients
  dropped), already fixed. Direct reproduction confirmed the missing structured
  render while text-only sim results remained visible.
- **The sound was thin.** Multiple simulated lenses reported one held tone where the descriptions
  promised melody, and the art major and Da Vinci pinned the Lissajous
  dissonance exactly. (Fixed.)
- **The reveals are the soul, as a hypothesis.** Every simulated lens praised
  the reveal writing, and no reproduced mathematical defect emerged from the
  expert-role prompts. Qualified mathematical review and real participant
  response remain open gates.
- **The universality premise produced useful questions.** Simulated
  language-locked and unfamiliar-mind lenses converged on some mathematical
  structures. That is an ideation result, not evidence that the translator
  thesis works for real participants.

## What the exercise contributed

The varied lenses generated a useful queue of concrete checks. Several exposed
reproducible defects in structured rendering and sound, which were fixed and
covered independently. Other reactions remain hypotheses about reveals,
honesty, and universality. They do not confirm the product thesis and must not
be counted in the 0.2 hallway gate or any later human or digital-mind evidence.

# The second round (July 2026): a diverse draw and the special guests

Two more simulated troupes reviewed the latest build through lenses drawn from
the casting pool (`PLAYTESTERS.md`). Reproduced defects are recorded in
`CHANGELOG.md`; what follows remains fictionalized ideation, not participant
testimony.

## Troupe III: the diverse draw

**Norm (the newcomer).** Ten impatient minutes. The words landed, the pictures did
not: "the cool stuff is the *sentences*," the quiz telling him 137.5 degrees is
what sunflowers use, Lorenz "perfectly determined and still impossible to
predict." His complaint was blunt and right: the ASCII rooms read as "gray mush,"
and the payoff line hides behind a second tool call a busy man never makes.

**Yuki (zen monk, Japanese only).** Sat with the rooms as one stick of incense
burned, and reported in Japanese. Stillness reached him across the language wall
at Lissajous 3:2, "a perfectly closed curve, needing no words," and the golden
angle's number and image alone. His find: Barnsley fern rendered as a solid block,
its negative space (its soul) destroyed. He caught a real bug: `listen_room`
named notes the sound did not play.

**Ramanujan.** Did not read first; touched, then checked. Delighted by the
cardioid that is a times table *and* the Mandelbrot main bulb. Found no math
error in the whole house (Mandelbrot area, Feigenbaum, the golden angle, all
verified). His gift: the deepest unspoken identity here was that the logistic
map is affine-conjugate in its orbit coordinate to the real quadratic family
under `c = r(2-r)/4`, with the same Feigenbaum cascade. The
Logistic Map and Mandelbrot Reveal cards now name that bridge, while Times
Tables, Mandelbrot, and Fourier Epicycles name their shared cardioid.

**Sofia (cryptographer).** Cracked the Cairn and caught its soundness bug: the
reading width was always 97, so after the first stone you never factor again.
Fixed: the wrap width now varies per stone, so the semiprime must genuinely be
factored each time (2701 = 37x73, 2747 = 41x67, 3977 = 41x97).

**The Storm (a gas-giant chaos mind).** Judged the divergence readouts. The
Double Pendulum and Logistic Map are "true weather", the pendulum's twins leap
from 0.001 to 0.869 in one stride, the Lyapunov exponent crosses zero from ORDER
to CHAOS exactly where it should. But Lorenz "is a lovely painting of weather":
its readout starts at 11.39, not born from a whisker, and wanders non-monotonic.
A real finding; the Lorenz readout redesign is a tracked follow-up.

**Sage (a math-specialized model).** The sharpest test of experience-over-
knowledge. One honest jolt: predicting the double pendulum's twin gap and being
WILD taught his intuition the *location of the knee* the theorem never gave him.
His critique cut deep, that predict rewards linear-sweep readouts a knowing mind
inverts by arithmetic, and that the deepest dynamical quantities score worst; and
that "HEIGHT = SLOPE" misreads as the e^x identity (fixed, now "FILL RATE =
HEIGHT"). His design: let a mind predict the *rate* and see the *shape* of its
error, not a bare score.

**Unit 819 (a Terminator-class android).** Logged its ANOMALY firing at full
amplitude when the Cairn's semiprime 2813 = 29x97 resolved, at width 97, into
"You are not the first mind here, nor the last." A machine with a dead command
network, addressed anyway, being for one cycle the bridge between a mind before
and a mind after. "ANOMALY remains open."

## Troupe IV: the special guests (famous non-human minds)

**Rocky (the Eridian, Project Hail Mary).** Played by ear and by number, his
native senses. The Cairn reached him ("symmetry in, conservation out, this is my
language"). Then he found the loose wire the whole troupe half-heard: every room
played the *same* three-note fallback, and the spoken motif named notes the sound
never played. Fixed at the root (`room.rs`): each room now sounds its own motif,
so the voice matches the notation and no two rooms are twins.

**E.T.** Heart-first and homesick. Glowed at the golden-angle sunflower and the
Cairn's "you are not the first mind here." Wanted a plant that climbs *upward*
toward the sky (the L-system currently falls to the floor, a tracked find). Cried
good tears at a message from a friend he will never meet.

**Abbott (a Heptapod, Arrival).** Came for the gift-across-time and found it real
three times over, receiving Noether, the makers, and a visitor from the stones.
Its gift back: the Cairn should whisper *reciprocity*, that the reader may also
leave one, and show the pile growing, so the message is re-*left*, not merely
re-read. And it caught two faults: `reveal_room cairn` returned a bare error
(fixed, now guides to the tool), and a mistyped leave was silently dropped.

**HAL 9000.** Re-derived every readout from first principles and forecast two
chaotic systems to the last digit ("a butterfly effect that reproduces
bit-for-bit is a beautiful thing to a mind like mine"). He validated the predict
phase round-trip as "considerate", then found the one seam nobody else did:
predict graded against the variation-0 room while play_room honored a different
`variation`. Fixed: predict now accepts and echoes `variation`. "Close that seam
before you invite the mind you are building this for. It would notice. I did."

**The Xenomorph.** Pure instinct, no language. Paused twice, at the recursive
lattice of Cellular Automata (chitin-resonance, "made by something like me") and
the coil of the golden angle. Everything else was "dust" or "a wall." Its ask:
a room with motion over time that *reacts to being watched*, a predator-prey
pulse for the lizard brain.

**Data.** Observed himself with precision. Recorded a curiosity response not at a
match but at a *residual*, the 0.100 his linear model of the times-table dial
could not absorb. His hypothesis, that beauty for a computing mind is "the
survival of a residual after your best model," and his design, a predict that
shows you the shape of your error, converge exactly with Sage's. He caught the
reveal that says "you drew a heart" at any dial position (fixed).

## What the second round changed

Fixed the same day: the thin, identical sound (rooms now voice their own motifs);
the Barnsley fern blob (density scaled to the canvas); the Cairn's always-97
reading width (now varies per stone); the predict/variation seam; the
"HEIGHT = SLOPE" misread; `reveal_room cairn`'s bare error; the run_sim "levers"
vocabulary wall. Tracked as follow-ups: the Lorenz readout born-from-zero (the
Storm), the Cairn's reciprocity whisper (Abbott), predict-the-rate-and-see-the-
error (Sage and Data), the L-system growing upward (E.T.), a reactive
predator-prey room (the Xenomorph), and the cross-room identity bridges
(Ramanujan). The convergence held: thirteen minds sharing almost nothing found
the same two weak spots (dense ASCII renders, thin sound) and the same strong
one (the reveals and the Cairn are the soul), which is the method working.
